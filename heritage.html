Heritage Locations in Sri lanka



Sri Lanka’s cultural depth is recognized by UNESCO, which has declared six archaeological World Heritage Sites in the country,

  • The sacred city of Anuradhapura
  • The golden temple of Dambulla
  • The ancient city of Polonnaruwa
  • The sacred city of Kandy
  • The ancient city of Sigiriya
  • The old town of Galle and its fortifications

The monumental dagobas (dome-shaped structures) and the remains of ancient buildings within the ruined cities of Anuradhapura and Polonnaruwa, as well as the amazing staircase to the temple at Dambulla and the sensual wall paintings of beautiful maidens at the palace on the rock of Sigiriya, will provide visitors with an unforgettable experience of these World Heritage Sites.

The Dalada Maligawa or Sacred Temple of the Tooth, which contains the Buddha's sacred tooth relic, is located in Kandy, Ceylon's final royal capital. With its unique design, art, and music, it is indeed rich in ancient culture.

Head south to Galle, you can find Asia's best-preserved Dutch fort, which was constructed in the mid-seventeenth century. With 14 large bastions, some old Dutch homes, a grid system of streets, the fort is full with life, much as it was when Galle was the major port in Sri Lanka. It is one of the most distinctive attractions in the country

Sigiriya



Sigiriya, also known as Lion Rock or Lion Mountain, is a location in central Sri Lanka that has the remnants of an ancient kingdom established in the late fifth century CE on a spectacular monolithic rock pillar. Dalada Maligawa(Temple Of The Tooth Relic)





The Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic, where Gautama Buddha's left tooth is housed, is a world-renowned destination of worship. UNESCO designated the temple, which is visited daily by hundreds of foreign and local visitors, as a world historic site in 1988.

The temple is located in Kandy, Central province, near the ancient Royal Palace to the north, and the woodland reserve is known as "Udawaththa forest" to the east. To the south, there the famed Kandy Lake is known as "Nuwara Weva," or "Kiri Muhuda" and to the west, "Natha & Paththini Devala."
