Sigiriya



Sigiriya, also known as Lion Rock or the Lion Fort, is an ancient fort city located in Sri Lanka. It was built and inhabited from around the 5th century AD and served as a palace and residence for King Kasyapa (also known as Kasyap V) of the Heroic Age of Buddhism. It is known as the 8th wonder of the world. The site was originally called "Singaraja". The name Sigiriya (lion rock) was given later when the site came under the influence of the Chola kingdom around the 9th-century ad.This rock rises to a height of 349 meters above sea level and 180 meters over the surrounding plain. Situated in the Matale district, The site is a world heritage site, as declared by UNESCO in 1982. The main site has maintained its original form except for minor alterations during various periods of time and is surrounded by ancient walls and moats. The climb to the top of Sigiriya rock from where you get an amazing 360° view over Sri Lanka was used as inspiration for the first ascent route up Everest.

History Of Sigiriya



The story behind the creation of this kingdom is tragic. There were two princes Mogallana and Kashyapa, Mogallana had been the heir to the throne, but his brother Kashyapa had seized control by force. By killing their father, the king. King Kashyapa was concerned that his brother might return to claim the kingdom for himself. Being afraid of Mogallana seeking revenge, Kashyapa constructed this castle to defend himself.

The Lion Rock is believed to be built in the place of Sigiri, the ancient city of Sri Lanka. During the period of King Kasyapa of the Hero Age, it was the place where he built his palace and laid the foundations of a cultural centre that was to become a great civilization. In the 5th century AD, the king built Sigiriya as a palace and a cultural centre. The Sigiriya rock was used as a building material for this palace. The rock was carved on all sides to provide a stairway with carvings depicting scenes from the ancient world. The Sigiriya rock carvings provide a rare glimpse into the ancient world of Sri Lanka.

Cultural Significance


The Sigiriya city was built on a massive rock and the surrounding plain was cultivated. This construction was done by cutting out the rock to create a large artificial platform on which the palace and other buildings were built. The architectural style of the buildings and the sculptures indicate that the site was developed by the people of the 4th or 5th century AD. Most of the structures have been damaged due to the natural weathering process and also by the human destructive activities and only scattered pieces remain.

Sigiriya is known for its exceptional artistic value,


Most popular artwork in Sigiriya is the potrayings of the Beautiful women. The drawings are said to be the lovely women that resided in King Kashyapa's court. They were known as "Sigiri Apsaras" Tourists are drawn to the paintings because of their facial expressions and the use of color. It denotes that the women are Queens of King Kashyapa. They appeared unearthly and were a representation of Kasyapa's grandeur. Also, these drawings are similarly similar to art seen in India's Ajanta Caves.



Sigiriya Graffiti contains the reflections of the fortress. People who have visited the place through the centuries have expressed their feelings the place is these walls. It consists of beautiful poems which also reflect historical richness in literature.
